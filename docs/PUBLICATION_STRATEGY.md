**Why:**
1. The special issue is closed, but the journal still actively publishes polycrisis research -- 5+ polycrisis articles in 2025-2026 outside the special issue.
2. Michael Lawrence remains Section Editor and the journal's identity is now tied to polycrisis research. A bibliometric mapping of the field he helped define would interest him editorially.
3. Open Access (CC-BY), no affiliation requirement, Cambridge University Press prestige.
4. IF 5.8 is strong for a first publication -- better than Sustainability (MDPI) without being unreachable.
5. The core finding (21/50 keyword overlap = convergence between polycrisis and LtG) is a genuinely novel empirical contribution -- no published bibliometric mapping of the polycrisis field exists.

**Realistic Timeline:**
- Weeks 1-3: Complete remaining analyses (citation bridge papers, temporal keyword evolution)

- Week 7: Post preprint on SSRN or ArXiv for priority

- **Total: ~6 months from today to published article (October 2026)**

**Editor to Target:** Michael Lawrence (Section Editor, Polycrisis). His PhD is from Balsillie School / University of Waterloo in global governance. He built Polycrisis.org. A bibliometric map of the field he is actively shaping is inherently useful to him.

**What's Needed to Execute:**

**Backup Plan:** If Global Sustainability rejects, immediately resubmit to Scientometrics (Springer, IF 3.2) or Sustainability (MDPI, IF 4.3). Both are fast reviewers and routinely publish bibliometric field mappings.

## Track B: Top-Tier Prestige Journal (Nature, Science, PNAS)

### Candidate Angles Evaluated

#### Angle 1: Live World3 Dashboard as Reproducible Model Validation
- **Pitch:** The World3 model has been validated by Herrington (2021) and recalibrated by Nebel et al. (2024), but all validations are static snapshots. The World3 Live Dashboard (world3-dashboard.streamlit.app) is the first continuously-updating, publicly-accessible validation tool with RMSE scoring against real data. This is a reproducibility contribution.
- **Target:** Journal of Industrial Ecology (where Herrington and Nebel published) or PNAS Nexus
- **Feasibility:** MEDIUM. The tool exists and works, but a dashboard alone is not a Nature-level contribution. It would need a novel finding -- e.g., "the BAU2 scenario now fits better than CT since 2022" or "here is the first real-time divergence detection between model and data."

#### Angle 2: Operationalizing "Polycrisis" Empirically
- **Pitch:** The term "polycrisis" remains conceptually contested (Rakowski et al. 2025 systematic review found only 59 rigorous papers out of 2,299). Use the bibliometric data + SDG regression clustering (Project 2 in research plan) to operationalize it: define polycrisis empirically via multi-domain simultaneous regression, then map which countries are in polycrisis.
- **Target:** Nature Sustainability or PNAS
- **Feasibility:** HIGH potential impact but requires 6-12 months of additional work beyond the bibliometric paper. This is a second paper, not a first.

#### Angle 3: Bridging Paper -- Polycrisis IS Modern LtG Overshoot Language
- **Pitch:** The 21/50 keyword overlap demonstrates that "polycrisis" and "limits to growth" are converging vocabularies for the same phenomenon. This is not just a bibliometric finding -- it is a conceptual argument that the polycrisis framework is the contemporary political language for what LtG modeled as overshoot. The Venn diagram is the proof.
- **Target:** Nature Sustainability (Comment or Perspective, 2,000-4,000 words)
- **Feasibility:** This is the strongest angle for a short, high-impact piece. A Nature Sustainability Comment (not a full article) does not require the same institutional weight. The argument is crisp, the data is ready, and it connects two major intellectual traditions.

### Honest Feasibility Assessment for Top Venues

**Without institutional affiliation:**
- Nature / Science full articles: Effectively impossible as a solo independent researcher. Editors desk-reject based on perceived credibility before peer review.
- Nature Sustainability full article: Very difficult solo. Acceptance rate ~5-8%. Desk rejection risk is high without a known name or institutional letterhead.
- Nature Sustainability Comment/Perspective: FEASIBLE. These are shorter (2,000-4,000 words), editorially selected (not full peer review), and more open to novel voices. The 21-keyword overlap finding is punchy enough for this format.
- PNAS Nexus: FEASIBLE. Open access, ~15% acceptance rate, 45-day first decision. Accepts "Independent Researcher" as affiliation. No member-sponsorship required (unlike PNAS proper).
- Global Environmental Change: Possible but slow (6-12 month review cycles at Elsevier).

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Global Sustainability rejects** | MEDIUM (30-40%) | HIGH -- delays everything | Immediate resubmit to Scientometrics or Sustainability (MDPI). Both are faster. |
| **APC cost ($3,450)** | HIGH | MEDIUM -- can't publish without paying | Check Cambridge fee waiver policy for independent researchers. Alternatively, Sustainability (MDPI) has fee waivers and lower APCs. Or submit to Scientometrics (Springer, hybrid -- can publish non-OA). |
| **No co-author responds** | MEDIUM | HIGH for Track B | Track A does not need a co-author. For Track B, cast wider: Jehn, Lawrence, Janzwood, Donges. If none respond, submit Nature Sustainability Comment solo -- it is feasible, just harder. |
| **Bibliometric paper is scooped** | LOW (no competing work found) | HIGH | Preprint on SSRN/ArXiv establishes priority. Post quickly. |
| **Dashboard is seen as "just a visualization"** | MEDIUM | MEDIUM for Track B | Frame it as infrastructure for reproducible science, not a standalone contribution. Pair with novel finding about scenario fit. |
| **Grad school apps rejected** | MEDIUM | HIGH | Apply broadly (5+ programs). Publication record is the strongest differentiator. Even a preprint under review is valuable. |

### Biggest Single Risk

**The APC cost.** $3,450 for Global Sustainability is a real barrier for an independent researcher with no institutional funding. If this is prohibitive, the strategy shifts to: (1) Scientometrics (Springer, hybrid model, can publish without OA fee), (2) Sustainability (MDPI, has fee waivers), or (3) negotiate with Cambridge directly -- some publishers waive fees for researchers in low-resource settings. Resolve this before submitting.
